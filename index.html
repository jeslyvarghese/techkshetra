Competition Rules

1. The game is played in a small court as a 3-on-3 match.

2. Barriers are placed around the court, and are to be factored into the players' strategy

3. Each match lasts 7 minutes

4. 32 teams contest in 2 pools for a grand prize of `4000. Second place winner gets `1500 and the maximum goal scorer gets `500.

5. Slots are allotted on a first come first serve basis.

Rules and Regulations

1. Each competing team must have two members

2. The event will be conducted in two rounds:

a. The first round will consist of an MCQ test, checking the contestants' knowledge of programming languages, algorithms and technologies. The top 10 teams will advance to the next round.

b. The second round will consist of two stages:

i. A tutorial on an esoteric programming language

ii. The contestants will then be given a problem, and asked to tackle it using the new programming language. They will then be asked to present it in front a panel of judges, and fellow contestants, who can then test the program and check for robustness

3. Judges decision is final

Circuit Debugging

Tell that electron where to go!

Delve deep into the inner working of that mass of wires, capacitors and semiconductors, get to that bug and fix it – before anyone else does.

Competition Rules

1. Contestants participate in teams of two
2. Event conducted in two phases:

a. Prelims
A written test of 30 minute duration, to test the contestants' basic electronics skills and ability to identify faults in the circuit.
b. Finals
Qualified teams will be provided with bugged hardware circuits, which they have to debug as fast as possible.

3. Contestants will be judged on the basis of speed of completion, quality of output and record of errors corrected. Decision of the judges is final.
